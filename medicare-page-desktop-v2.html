No Preset Limits

How Many Therapy Sessions Does Medicare Pay For?

Medicare Advantage plans have no preset annual limits on therapy sessions. Coverage is based on medical necessity as determined by your therapist's clinical documentation.

Think of therapy like dental care ‚Äì you don't stop brushing because a cavity healed. Many of our Medicare patients start with a specific challenge and stay because consistent support keeps them sharper, more resilient, and more connected. There is no preset limit, and many patients choose to continue therapy as a proactive investment in their mental health.

Our senior care therapists use evidence-based approaches proven effective for older adults:

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) adapted for seniors
  • Problem-Solving Therapy (PST) focused on daily challenges
  • Interpersonal Therapy (IPT) for relationship and role changes
  • Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) for values-based living in later life
  • Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction adapted for age-related stressors

Have Original Medicare With a Supplement (Medigap)?

If you carry a Medigap plan, therapy costs are often predictable and very low. Medigap plans cover the 20% coinsurance that Original Medicare leaves to the patient. Some plans also cover the annual Part B deductible.

Here's the simple version: Plans B, C, and F cover everything ‚Äì $0 out of pocket. Plans D, G, M, and A require only the annual Part B deductible ($257 in 2026). Plan N adds the deductible plus a $20 copay per session. Plan L covers the deductible plus 25% coinsurance, and Plan K covers the deductible plus 50% coinsurance.

Plans C and F are only available if you became Medicare-eligible before January 1, 2020. Plan G is currently the most common Medigap plan and provides excellent mental health coverage once the deductible is met.

Not sure which Medigap letter you carry? We'll check your plan and tell you exactly what you'll pay before you schedule. Call 212-960-8626.
